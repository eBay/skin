@ebay/skin/carousel

Carousels group together a list of similar items into a horizontal viewport of fixed height. Items may be entirely static (e.g. an image), entirely interactive (e.g. button) or contain a mix of static and interactive elements (e.g. a heading, image and link).

Items may be grouped and paginated via a discrete number of slides, or left ungrouped and paginated in a continuous manner.

NOTE: No JavaScript behaviour has been implemented in the carousel examples (i.e. the controls are non-functional), we demonstrate the CSS aspect only. For detailed behaviour requirements, please visit the eBay MIND Pattern for Carousel.

Filmstrip Carousel (Continuous)

Any type of carousel showing more than one item per slide is known as a filmstrip carousel. This is analogous to looking at a strip of photographic film. By default, most carousels fall into this category.

Continuous carousels always require a "peek" to give affordance of more content and carousel behaviour.

Filmstrip Carousel (Discrete)

When grouping filmstrip items into discrete slides (e.g. 4 items per slide in the example below), add the carousel--slides modifier.

A discrete carousel must give some visual affordance to the total number of slides. This can be achieved with a strip of pagination "dots" underneath the carousel.

A discrete carousel requires an ARIA live-region to convey slide updates to assistive technology.

Slideshow Carousel

A discrete carousel showing exactly one item per slide is known as a slideshow carousel. This is analogous to an old fashioned slide projector, viewing one image at a time.

A slideshow carousel should have no peek, therefore all items must be set to 100% width.
